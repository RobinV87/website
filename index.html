Let's go a bit deeper now, and do like the commands say, list out who I am and make a directory of who I am and what I do. We will have to start at the beginning, way back when a 1.44MB floppy disk could destroy the world, I was 8 years old and my dad had bought a MSDOS computer.

I was pretty good in learning the commands, I believe I had this little cheat sheet with hos to traverse the file structure that we had. Loading up my favrotie games like:

  • Commander Keen
  • Doom
  • Duke Nukem
  • Jazz Jackrabbit
  • And many more!

When We soon went on to Windows 3.1, it felt like a new world, no more stuffy command line, that was such a big part of me growing up. And then came Windows 95, the first time I got into contact with The Internet! The modem that hogged the telephone line and you had to pay per minute to use it. But the 56KB Dial Up Modem was not reliable at all, and with parents who thought it was all magic and couldn't understand it, 8 year old me started fixing our Dial Up Modem. If there would ever be a book written about me, that should be my origin story, sitting on the telephone with support agents who were teaching me everything, even stuff I still use in my job right now!

A couple of years later I got some friends who were as much into computers as me and we decided to learn how to abuse the horrible password security to be able to...access certain websites. We also started downloading anything we wanted and even selling games on Floppy disks. Gotta love flee markets where anything could be sold and we could buy more Pokemon cards!

The internet got faster, ADSL was the next big thing, and my friend even got Satelite internet, it felt like we were in the future! Online gaming became a thing and I played Tony Hawk Pro Skater online with people. Thinking back on it, It was horribly choppy and slow, but it was possible. You blink two times and you have gigabit internet and you can play Dungeons and Dragons with people all over the world!

And the fun thing is, we are only just starting!
